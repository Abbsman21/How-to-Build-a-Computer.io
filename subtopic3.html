 Want a Better PC? Try Building Your Own
Site Author
 Jaine Grey and Louryn Strampe
Abstract
 This Wired article explains how to build a computer with clear but detailed step-by-step instructions. the article covers the entire process from selecting components to completing the entire build, it starts off with the necessary parts such as the CPU, RAM, Motherboard, Graphics card, power supply, and devices such as Mouse and Keyboard, the article also explains the value of compatible computer parts since if you get mismatched parts, your build will not go well at all, the article also offers diagrams and useful tip and it highlights important techniques and precautions since these parts can be very delicate. In the end, this guide serves as a great tool for anyone interested and looking into building their own computers.

 Why Building Your Own PC Is Still a Smart Move
Site Author
 Avram Piltch
Abstract
 This article explores the advantages of building your computer. It goes into the various factors such as cost, optimization options, performance, and satisfaction. It starts off with an analysis of the cost aspect and how you can potentially save money over spending a lot of money. It also addresses some misinformation that goes around in the tech world and concerns surrounding building your computer instead of buying one. The article also provides real-world examples and even expert opinions, the article helps with deciding whether you want to build a computer or buy one with important information that ultimately helps the reader make an informed decision.
